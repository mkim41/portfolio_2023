INHERENT VICE


CREATING IMMERSIVE AND ENGAGING GRAPHICS FOR PERSISHING MOMENTS OF GILDED AGE



Inherent Vice, the 2022 RISD Museum exhibit, aims to make the behind-the-scenes of the museum more accessible to the public by displaying a series of damaged gowns from America’s Gilded Age (1870s–1910s) soon to be deaccessioned. The exhibit also showcased apparel students' work who took the course named after the exhibit and engaged with the gowns by creatively responding to the prompt “hidden stories.”

I was responsible for designing the identity, motion, environmental graphics, and label designs for the exhibit.

For the main typeface, I chose Voyage by VJ Type to reflect the glamorous Gilded Age and excessively ornamental nature of the gowns from the period. Swear Text by OH NO Type Company was chosen as the secondary typeface for high contrast and curves that matched Voyage.

Taking the inspiration from damaged gowns and their debris, I created the motion for the identity where the the text "Inherent Vice," breaks into sequin like particles and falls down.

Two label designs were created—one for the wall to caption students' works, and the other for the large scale case where the Gilded Age gowns are displayed. The case label included both mateiral and historical context around each dresses on display.

The textile gallery for Inherent Vice exhibit had three projectors that could maximize the walls and escalate the space.

The first projector showed the primary exhibit identity as well as introduction to the exhibit, while the second projector displayed the works of Pablo Genoves. Genoves' post apocalyptic art that deconstructs the historical buildings accentuated the mood for the exhibit. The third projection magnified the photographs of decaying dresses for the first half of the exhibit duration, then switched to show the apparel students' works inspired by the dresses.
